Building a Ray Tracer from Scratch

January 2026 • 15 min read • C++

Figure 1: Most recent render with diffuse illumination

Ray tracing has always fascinated me as the holy grail of computer graphics. While modern GPUs now offer hardware-accelerated ray tracing, I wanted to understand the fundamentals by building my own from scratch. This project took me three months of weekends and late nights, but the results were worth it.

Why Build a Ray Tracer?

Most 3D graphics today use rasterization, which projects 3D objects onto a 2D screen. While fast, rasterization struggles with realistic lighting effects. Ray tracing, in contrast, simulates the physical behavior of light, creating stunningly realistic images with accurate shadows, reflections, and refractions.
